PureFrame is a semantic video search API that lets you upload videos, index their visual and audio content, and then query them with natural language text or reference images to retrieve precise, timestamped clips. Instead of relying on file names or manual tags, PureFrame understands what's _happening_ in your videos — so you can find exactly the moment you need, at scale.

## What you can build

PureFrame fits anywhere you need to make video content discoverable or actionable:

- **Video search in apps** — Let users search a video library by describing what they want to see, returning the exact clip rather than a file title.
- **Content moderation clip retrieval** — Retrieve specific segments flagged by policy categories without scrubbing through full recordings.
- **Visual search** — Upload a reference image and find every video segment where a matching scene, object, or person appears.
- **AI agent memory** — Give your agents a long-term video memory store they can query to recall visual context from past sessions or recordings.
- **Media asset management** — Automatically surface stock footage, archival clips, or branded assets by semantic description rather than manual metadata.

## How it works

Getting from raw video to searchable clips takes three steps:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload">
    Send your video file to PureFrame along with the collection you want it to belong to. PureFrame returns a `video_id` and a `job_id` immediately so your application can continue without blocking.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Index">
    PureFrame processes your video in the background — extracting frames, transcribing audio, and building a semantic index. You can poll the job status endpoint or use a webhook to be notified the moment indexing is complete.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search">
    Submit a text query or reference image against a collection. PureFrame returns a ranked list of matching video segments, each with a `timestamp_start`, `timestamp_end`, relevance score, and direct clip URL.
  </Step>
</Steps>
